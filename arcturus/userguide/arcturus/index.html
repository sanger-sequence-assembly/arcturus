Introduction to Arcturus

This document introduces you to the Arcturus system and gives brief answers to the following questions:
  • What is Arcturus?
  • What does it do?
  • How do we use it?

What is Arcturus?

Arcturus is a software system which was developed in the Pathogen Sequencing Unit (PSU) of the Sanger Institute to manage eukaryotic whole-genome sequencing projects.  It was initially created to support the sequencing of protozoan parasites such as Leishmania and Trypanosoma and helminths such as Schistosoma, but it is now also routinely used in bacterial sequencing projects.

Arcturus uses a relational database system to store information about read sequences and contigs.

Contigs can be exported from Arcturus to a Gap4 database for editing, and Gap4 databases can be imported into Arcturus.

Arcturus maintains information about successive "generations" of contigs throughout the lifetime of a sequencing project.

Users interact with Arcturus using a front-end software package called Minerva.

What does it do?

Arcturus allows several finishers to work together on the same sequencing project.

Arcturus groups contigs into sets called projects.  Each project may correspond to a chromosome, or a clone, or any region of the genome which is being worked on by a finisher.

Each project has a named owner who can control the contigs which it contains.

Arcturus gives annotators access to unfinished sequence at an early stage.

Annotators are encouraged to begin analysing a new genome as soon as contigs become available from the shotgun assembly.  In the past, this has led to problems later in the lifetime of the sequencing project when preliminary annotations had to be re-mapped to contigs at a more advanced stage of finishing.

Arcturus avoids such problems by automatically re-mapping annotations to each new generation of contigs as they are imported.

Arcturus maintains a complete history of a sequencing project.

Arcturus retains information about all of the contigs that are imported, from the very first shotgun assembly to the finished genome.

Arcturus gives each contig a unique identification code.

Gap4 contig names are not unique or permanent.  They cannot be used to identify a contig over the lifetime of a sequencing project.  This has led to major problems, such as when external collaborators identify a gene of interest in a particular contig in a public data release, then return to it many months later, and ask us about the contig using its Gap4 name.  It may be difficult, or even impossible, to identify the correct contig, since Gap4 names are transient.

Arcturus solves this problem by assigning a unique and permanent identification code to every contig.

Arcturus provides finishers, annotators and project managers with useful tools.

Finishers can search for specific reads or oligo sequences within their projects, carry out directed assemblies of finishing reads, construct and display scaffolds of contigs linked by pUC bridges, and transfer contigs between projects.

Annotators can export contigs in EMBL or FASTA format and examine the history of specific annotations.

Project managers can track the progress of a sequencing project at several levels, from individual projects to the entire genome.  They can also assign projects to finishers.

How do we use it?

You will normally interact with Arcturus via a front-end software package called Minerva which allows you to manage your projects and contigs through an easy-to-use program which runs on your desktop or laptop.
